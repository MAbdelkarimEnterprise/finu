DESKTOP INTERACTION

When no tile is active:

- All tiles remain readable.
- The ambient Midnight Intelligence shader or gradient field is visible behind the grid.
- Tiles have quiet depth, thin borders, and small animated FINU-native product symbols.

When a tile is hovered or keyboard-focused:

- The active tile becomes completely sharp.
- It rises approximately 6–10 pixels.
- It may scale up slightly, with a maximum around 1.02–1.035.
- Its border illumination increases.
- Its product symbol becomes brighter and more active.
- Its arrow or action indicator resolves from scattered dots into a directional arrow.
- The background and non-active tiles blur slightly.
- Non-active tiles reduce opacity to approximately 40–60%.
- Non-active tiles may scale down very slightly.
- The active tile’s local light field follows the pointer with damping.

The focus effect should transition smoothly over approximately 400–650ms using FINU’s existing easing.

Do not apply such strong blur that users lose spatial orientation. The active tile should be unmistakable, but the complete grid must remain understandable.

When the pointer leaves the complete grid:

- All cards smoothly return to their neutral state.
- Blur, opacity, and scale reset without snapping.

KEYBOARD BEHAVIOR

Keyboard focus must produce the same hierarchy as hover:

- A focused card becomes sharp and elevated.
- Sibling cards soften.
- Enter activates the link.
- Escape closes the explorer.
- Focus is trapped inside the open explorer.
- Focus returns to the menu trigger after closing.

MOBILE BEHAVIOR

Do not reproduce the large desktop blur choreography on touch devices.

On mobile:

- Use a clean stacked or two-column tile layout.
- Keep every tile sharp and readable.
- Make Finu AI the first and largest tile.
- Use tap feedback, active borders, and icon motion.
- Maintain 44×44-pixel minimum controls.
- Prevent horizontal overflow.
